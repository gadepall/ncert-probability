\textbf{Question 10.13.3.36}\\
A carton of 24 bulbs contain 6 defective bulbs. One bulbs is drawn at random.
What is the probability that the bulb is not defective? If the bulb selected is defective
and it is not replaced and a second bulb is selected at random from the rest, what
is the probability that the second bulb is defective?\\
\solution
\fi
Let 
\begin{align}
	 X =
	\begin{cases}
		1, & \text{if bulb is not defective}\\ 
		0, & \text{if bulb is defective}
	\end{cases}
\end{align}
Then  
\begin{align}
               p_X\brak{0} &= \frac{6}{24}\\
               &=\frac{1}{4}\\
               p_X\brak{1} &= 1-p_X\brak{0}\\
               &=1-\frac{1}{4}\\
               &=\frac{3}{4}          
\end{align}
When a defective bulb is selected and not replaced
\begin{align}
	 Y =
	\begin{cases}
		1, & \text{if bulb is not defective}\\ 
		0, & \text{if bulb is defective}
	\end{cases}
\end{align}
Then
\begin{align}
p_Y\brak{0} &= \frac{6-1}{24-1}\\
&=\frac{5}{23}
\end{align}
